⚔ A Foul Call ⚔
CATEGORY: Narrative Design
INVESTIGATION PATHS: 3
⚔ QUEST SUMMARY
Kliq, a seasoned bounty hunter, arrives in Stone-Upon-Fleen to meet an old ally: Reverend Lemuel, a mysterious priest from a secret order. Lemuel's letter speaks of foul omens and strange happenings in the village. What begins as a reunion becomes an investigation into dark magic, moral choices, and secrets that will test Kliq's loyalty to both friend and principle.
The Call
Lemuel has written to Kliq about bad omens plaguing Stone-Upon-Fleen. The village is experiencing bizarre phenomena: missing pumpkins, a foul stench, and villagers falling mysteriously ill. Kliq must meet Lemuel and uncover the truth behind these events—a truth far darker than simple misfortune.

⚔ THREE PATHS TO DISCOVERY ⚔
🔍 HOW PLAYERS INVESTIGATE
A Foul Call offers three distinct investigation approaches. Each leads to the same truth but shapes how Kliq discovers it and which NPCs he encounters along the way.
🎯
The Direct Path
Kliq goes straight to Lemuel. The reverend immediately reveals the foul smell emanating from Clare's garden, cutting through investigation and moving directly to action.
⏱️ Context Heavy route
Flow: Speak to Lemuel → Investigate Garden → Report Findings → Go to Barn → Fight Spirit → Consequences
🔎
The Investigation Path
Kliq interviews townsfolk about three mysterious events: missing pumpkins, foul smell, and sickness from rotten mushrooms. Each NPC provides a voice line that adds a discovery to the journal, painting a picture of something wrong.
📊 Discovery heavy route
Flow: Gather 3 Clues → Speak to Lemuel → Get Keys → Go to Barn → Fight Spirit → Consequences
💪
The Onslaught Path
Kliq bypasses investigation entirely and goes directly to the barn. Using the Onslaught attribute (a skill player has invested points in), he breaks the barn door and confronts the spirit head-on, skipping all preceding story beats.
⚔️ Fastest Route
Flow: Go to Barn → Break Door → Fight Spirit → Consequences

Kliq has discovered the ritual site but needs the key to the barn where the spirit is imprisoned.

Kliq tells Lemuel about the amateurish ritual. Lemuel trusts Kliq's judgment and gives him the key without pressing for details. This preserves Lemuel's trust in Kliq's autonomy.

Kliq discovers Kathlyn's name and address in the book and seeks her out. "Who even writes their name and address on a dark spell rituals book?" says Kliq. She also possesses a key to the barn. This path creates a new alliance and provides alternative access.

« CONTEXT-AWARE DIALOGUE »
💬 MEETING CLARE: DIALOGUE CHANGES WITH KNOWLEDGE
When Kliq reaches Clare in the barn, his opening dialogue shifts based on what he's learned along the way. Same meeting, different conversation. This creates micro-variations that feel organic to the journey taken.
🎯 SCENARIO 1: The Direct Approach
Kliq went straight to Lemuel, got the barn key, and came directly here. He knows nothing about Clare except that a ritual happened.
🎬 Barn Meeting (Direct)
(images/barn-direct-approach.gif)
KLIQ:
"Who are you?"
Analysis: Kliq has no context. He doesn't know her name, her involvement, or her situation. His question is direct and probing—he's gathering information.
CLARE:
"I'm Clare. I... I was trying to win the competition."
Impact: Clare's answer is defensive and explanatory. She assumes Kliq doesn't know anything, so she offers information freely to justify herself.
🔍 SCENARIO 2: Investigated the Garden
Kliq investigated Clare's garden, found the ritual site, and either followed the vines or talked to Foy. He knows the ritual happened in her garden but hasn't spoken to Kathlyn.
🎬 Barn Meeting (Garden Knowledge)
(images/barn-garden-approach.gif)
KLIQ:
"I was in your garden. I saw what you did. What's your name?"
Analysis: Kliq has evidence. He confronts her with what he knows, showing he's done his homework. His question now has weight—he already knows the ritual was hers.
CLARE:
"I'm Clare. Please... you have to understand, I wasn't trying to hurt anyone."
Impact: Clare is caught. She knows Kliq saw the garden. Her response shifts to defensiveness and pleading. She's no longer offering information—she's asking for mercy.
🎭 SCENARIO 3: Spoke to Kathlyn First
Kliq found Kathlyn's name in the book, tracked her down, and spoke with her. He knows both conspirators' names and that they worked together. He arrives at the barn with full knowledge.
🎬 Barn Meeting (Full Knowledge)
(images/barn-full-knowledge.gif)
KLIQ:
"Kathlyn told me everything, Clare. I know what you both did."
Analysis: Kliq cuts through all pretense. He has the full picture. His statement is direct and reveals he's already gathered all the information. The dialogue is shorter because there's nothing left to discover.
CLARE:
"... Yes. We both did this. Kathlyn wanted to help me."
Impact: Clare's response is resigned. She knows there's no hiding. The dialogue is cut short—she acknowledges reality rather than defend or explain. This version feels more honest and less performative.

Lemuel asks what Kliq discovered. The truth about Clare and Kathlyn's involvement now hangs in the balance. Kliq can reveal nothing, partial truth, or full truth.
🎭 Final Lemuel Conversation
(images/lemuel-final.gif)
OPTION 1: Tell Nothing

✓ BEST ENDING: Lemuel has said he trusts what Kliq is doing and won't press for details. This preserves everyone's secret and shows wisdom in discretion. Both Clare and Kathlyn remain in the village, protected.

Consequence: Both face potential judgment. Lemuel must deal with both parties. Full transparency, but also full responsibility for the consequences.

🎬 OUTCOMES & CONSEQUENCES
✓ Best Ending: Discretion
Kliq tells Lemuel nothing about Clare or Kathlyn. The secret is kept. Both women remain in the village. Clare is grateful for being saved and becomes an ally. Lemuel respects Kliq's judgment.
Future Quest: Clare can be called upon to help Kliq in a future mission. Lemuel's trust deepens.
✗ Worst Ending: Exposure
Kliq tells Lemuel only about Clare, protecting Kathlyn. Lemuel has no choice but to enforce village justice. Clare is banished from Stone-Upon-Fleen. She is exiled for her crime.
Clare loses her home and life. Kathlyn remains secret, creating a debt to Kliq. A dark ending born from incomplete mercy.
📋 Full Truth: Both Exposed
Kliq reveals both Clare and Kathlyn's involvement. Lemuel must address both. The truth is laid bare, and justice—whatever form it takes—falls on both conspirators.
Full transparency but complex consequences. Both NPCs' fates are uncertain. Lemuel makes the final judgment.
⚔️ Stealth Kill Ending
If Kliq has high Stealth, he kills the pumpkin spirit without a fight. No witnesses. Clare is freed silently. Everything proceeds as normal, but Kliq solved it alone.
Lemuel never knows about the barn. No one saw the truth. Kliq's method remains his secret. A ghost resolution.
⚔ DESIGN PHILOSOPHY ⚔
💡 CORE DESIGN THINKING
Player Agency Through Attributes
Every skill point a player invests (Discovery, Charisma, Onslaught, Stealth) directly unlocks narrative paths. A combat-focused character experiences the quest differently from an investigation-focused one. This makes character progression feel consequential to story.
Moral Ambiguity Over Judgment
The quest doesn't judge the player for their choices. Remaining silent isn't "good" and honesty isn't "pure good." Each path has wisdom and cost. Clare is a victim of her own ambition but still a sympathetic character. Kathlyn is complicit but mysterious. Players must navigate nuance, not alignment.
Multiple Truths
The three investigation paths (Direct, Clue-Gathering, Onslaught) arrive at the same ritual site but with different understandings of the context. A player who gathers clues understands the village's suffering. A direct player is spared that emotional weight. Both are valid experiences.
Consequences That Ripple Forward
Choices in A Foul Call affect future quests (Clare's alliance), NPC availability (her potential banishment), and trust relationships (Lemuel's trust in Kliq's judgment). The world remembers what Kliq did.
Accessibility Through Flexibility
A stealth-focused player can bypass the entire investigation and confrontation. A combat player can Onslaught the barn door. A diplomatic player can charm Foy. Every playstyle has a path to victory. No player is locked out of the quest's core experience.
